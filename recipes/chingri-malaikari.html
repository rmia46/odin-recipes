Bengali Cuisine » Chingri Malai Kari

Bengali Chingri Malai Kari is a delightful and popular dish from the Bengali cuisine, known for its rich, creamy texture and aromatic flavors. This recipe combines succulent prawns (chingri) with a luscious coconut milk (malai) curry, creating a perfect balance of spices and creaminess. It is typically served with steamed rice or naan bread, making it a satisfying and flavorful meal.

Ingredients

  • 500 grams of prawns (cleaned and deveined)
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 2 onions (finely chopped)
  • 2 tomatoes (finely chopped)
  • 2 green chilies (slit)
  • 2 teaspoons ginger paste
  • 2 teaspoons garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnishing)

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a deep pan or kadai over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and saut√© until they turn golden brown.
  2. Add the ginger paste and garlic paste to the pan and cook for a minute until the raw smell disappears.
  3. Add the chopped tomatoes and green chilies. Cook until the tomatoes become soft and mushy.
  4. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, cumin powder, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for a few minutes.
  5. Reduce the heat to low and add the cleaned prawns to the pan. Stir gently to coat the prawns with the spice mixture.
  6. Pour the coconut milk into the pan and stir well. Simmer the curry for about 10-15 minutes until the prawns are cooked through and the flavors are well blended.
  7. Sprinkle garam masala over the curry and give it a final mix.
  8. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  9. Serve the Bengali Chingri Malai Kari hot with steamed rice or naan bread.

Cautions

  • Make sure the prawns are fresh and properly cleaned before cooking.
  • Adjust the spice levels according to your taste preferences.
  • Be careful not to overcook the prawns, as they can become tough and rubbery.
  • Use caution when handling hot pans and cooking utensils to avoid burns.

Enjoy your meal and savor the flavors of this authentic Bengali dish!
