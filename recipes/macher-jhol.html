Bengali Cuisine » Macher Jhol

Bengali Macher Jhol is a beloved fish curry that represents the heart and soul of Bengali cuisine. It is a light and flavorful dish, known for its delicate balance of spices and the freshness of the fish. Macher Jhol is typically prepared with a variety of fish, such as Rohu, Katla, or Hilsa, and is enjoyed with steamed rice.

Ingredients

  • 500 grams fish (Rohu, Katla, or any other fish of your choice)
  • 2 tablespoons mustard oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ginger paste
  • 1 teaspoon garlic paste
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • Salt to taste
  • Water for gravy
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Steps

  1. Clean and wash the fish thoroughly. Marinate the fish pieces with turmeric powder and salt. Set aside for 15 minutes.
  2. Heat mustard oil in a pan or kadhai over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Add the chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
  4. Add the ginger and garlic paste and sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears.
  5. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and mushy.
  6. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well to combine the spices with the onion-tomato mixture.
  7. Gently place the marinated fish pieces into the pan and coat them with the onion-tomato spice mixture.
  8. Add water to the pan, covering the fish completely. Bring the curry to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for about 10-15 minutes until the fish is cooked through and the flavors meld together.
  9. Add slit green chilies and simmer for another minute.
  10. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  11. Serve the Macher Jhol hot with steamed rice.

Cautions

  • Be cautious while handling the fish to avoid any injuries or contamination. Ensure that the fish is fresh and cleaned properly before using.
  • Adjust the amount of spices and chilies according to your taste preferences. Start with smaller quantities and gradually increase as desired.
  • Keep an eye on the cooking time to avoid overcooking the fish, as it could result in a loss of texture and flavor.
  • Use caution when working with hot oil to prevent any accidents. Handle the pan with oven mitts or protective equipment.

Congratulations on trying out this classic Bengali recipe! Enjoy the delightful flavors of Macher Jhol and the warm comfort it brings to your meal.
