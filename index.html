Bengali Cuisine

Bengali cuisine is known for its rich and diverse flavors, combining a wide range of spices, herbs, and ingredients. It is a culinary tradition that originates from the Bengal region in South Asia, which includes present-day Bangladesh and the Indian state of West Bengal. Bengali cuisine offers a variety of dishes that cater to both vegetarian and non-vegetarian preferences. Rice and fish are staple ingredients in Bengali meals, and mustard oil is commonly used for cooking. The cuisine is characterized by its use of aromatic spices such as cumin, coriander, turmeric, ginger, and mustard seeds.
